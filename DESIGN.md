## 1. Executive Summary

ECONLAB is a suite of 26 independent, browser-based interactive lab applications designed to accompany introductory economics instruction at the community-college level. The project spans two courses — ECO 210 (Macroeconomics) and ECO 211 (Microeconomics) — both aligned to the OpenStax *Principles of Macroeconomics* 3e and *Principles of Microeconomics* 3e textbooks respectively. Each lab corresponds to one or more textbook chapters and replaces passive reading with active station-based practice: students classify, calculate, explore real data, and ultimately demonstrate mastery on a timed quiz before the lab is marked complete.

The scope is: 26 lab apps (14 for ECO 210, 12 for ECO 211) plus 2 course hub dashboards. Labs are organized into chapter labs, review labs, and a practice final exam for each course. The chapter labs contain 5–8 interactive stations covering one or two OpenStax chapters, culminating in a 10-question quiz that requires 9/10 correct for mastery. Review labs cover multi-chapter spans and require 13/15 for mastery. Practice finals draw 30 questions from a bank of 43–57 items and carry no mastery gate — they serve as diagnostic preparation for the summative exam.

The pedagogical approach rests on three pillars. First, mastery gating: students cannot submit a completed lab to Brightspace until they pass the quiz at the required threshold, which directs effort toward genuine understanding rather than mere completion. Second, spaced retrieval: every chapter lab (starting with Ch7) opens with a 5-question recap of the prior chapter, so knowledge from earlier labs is re-tested before new content is introduced. Third, active formula derivation: calculator stations — for GDP computation, unemployment rates, CPI inflation, the Keynesian multiplier, money multiplier, cost curves, profit maximization, and HHI — require students to produce numeric answers from raw data rather than select a recalled fact. The tech stack is Vite + React 18 + TypeScript + Tailwind CSS + Radix UI, with Recharts or custom SVG for data visualization; each lab is a standalone SPA deployed independently via Perplexity Computer Apps.

## 4. Pedagogical Design Principles

### Station-Based Learning with Mastery Gating

Each chapter lab presents content through 5–8 discrete stations, each covering a distinct concept or skill. The quiz station is locked behind a padlock icon until all content stations are marked complete. Completing a station requires the student to engage with the content — classifying all items, answering all check-in questions, solving all calculator problems, or revealing all accordion panels — not merely viewing the page. Passing the quiz at the mastery threshold (9/10 for chapter labs, 13/15 for review labs) is required before `localStorage` sets the completion flag and the Course Hub shows `✓ Completed`.

### Spaced Retrieval Through Recap Stations

Beginning with Ch7 in both courses, every chapter lab opens with a 5-question Recap station that tests concepts from the immediately preceding chapter. This creates a spaced-retrieval loop: a student working through Ch8 will re-encounter Ch7 concepts (Rule of 70, four ingredients of growth, NAIRU) before engaging with Ch8 content (BLS classification, unemployment types, U-3 formula). Recap stations must be marked complete before the quiz unlocks; they are not graded separately but are counted toward the station-completion gate.

### Active Formula Derivation

Several labs include calculator stations that require typed numeric answers rather than multiple-choice recognition. Examples include:

- **GDP Calculator (Ch6):** `Real GDP = (Nominal GDP ÷ Price Index) × 100`
- **Rule of 70 Calculator (Ch7):** `Years to double = 70 ÷ growth rate`
- **Formula Lab (Ch8):** `U-3 = Unemployed ÷ Labor Force × 100` and `LFPR = Labor Force ÷ Working-Age Pop × 100`
- **CPI Math (Ch9):** `CPI = (Current Cost ÷ Base Cost) × 100` and `Inflation = (CPI_new − CPI_old) ÷ CPI_old × 100`
- **Multiplier Calculator (Ch12+13):** `Multiplier = 1 ÷ (1 − MPC)` and `ΔGDP = Multiplier × ΔG`
- **T-Account Explorer (Ch14):** `Money Multiplier = 1 ÷ Reserve Requirement`
- **Cost Curve Lab (Ch7 ECO 211):** `TC = FC + VC`, `MC = ΔTC / ΔQ`, `ATC = TC / Q`
- **Profit Calculator (Ch7 ECO 211):** `Economic Profit = Accounting Profit − Implicit Costs`
- **HHI Calculator (Ch11 ECO 211):** `HHI = Σ(market share²)`

Calculator stations use tolerance-based checking (e.g., ±15 for GDP, ±0.2% for CPI rates) and a three-attempt scaffold: attempt 1 wrong → "Not quite"; attempt 2 → hint shown; attempt 3+ → answer revealed with explanation. This prevents students from being permanently blocked while still requiring genuine effort.

### Real-World Data Integration via FRED

Six of the 14 ECO 210 labs include interactive FRED data charts that display historical macroeconomic series:

| Lab | FRED Series | Data Range | Data Points |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ch 6 | Nominal GDP (GDPA) + Real GDP (GDPCA) | 1947–2025 | 79 annual points each |
| Ch 7 | Real GDP per Capita (A939RX0Q048SBEA) | 1947–2025 | 79 annual points |
| Ch 8 | Unemployment Rate (UNRATE) | Jan 1948–Feb 2026 | ~930 monthly points |
| Ch 9 | CPI YoY % (CPIAUCSL) | Jan 1948–Feb 2026 | ~930 monthly points |
| Ch 11 | Output Gap + Potential GDP (GDPPOT) | 1949–2025 | Annual |
| Ch 14 | M2 Money Supply | 1980–2024 | 45 annual points |
| Ch 15 | Federal Funds Rate | 1954–2024 | Annual averages |
| Ch 17 | Federal Debt as % of GDP | 1940–2024 | 85 annual points |

All FRED data is hard-coded as inline arrays in each lab's `EconLab.tsx` source file. There are no runtime API calls. NBER recession bands (12 recessions) are also hard-coded and rendered as shaded rectangles on the time-series charts.

The FRED stations use a locked-tooltip pattern: the crosshair is always visible so students can read approximate values, but the full data tooltip is hidden until all guided questions in that station are answered — preventing students from simply reading answers off the chart.

### Question Randomization

All quiz stations shuffle their answer-option order on each attempt using a Fisher-Yates algorithm. Several labs also draw from larger question pools (e.g., Ch2 draws 10 from 11; the ECO 210 practice final draws 30 from 57). This reduces the effectiveness of answer memorization across retakes and encourages genuine learning rather than pattern recognition.

### localStorage Progress Persistence

Each lab writes station completion state to `localStorage` using lab-specific keys (e.g., `econlab_done_ch6`, `econlab211_done_ch10`). The Course Hub reads these keys to display completion checkmarks without any server-side state. Because `localStorage` is browser-scoped and origin-scoped, progress persists across sessions on the same device and browser but does not sync across devices.

### PDF Export as Assessment Artifact

On passing the quiz, students reach a Results screen that includes a name-input field, an optional exit-ticket textarea, a per-question review table, and a "Print PDF" button. The PDF is generated via `window.open()` + `document.write()` + `window.print()` into a new browser tab, producing a print-ready HTML page that students save as a PDF and submit to Brightspace. The PDF includes: student name, date, score, grade label, per-question results with explanations, and (for review labs) a section-score breakdown table.

## 5. Technical Architecture

### Stack

Each of the 26 labs is an independent Vite + React 18 + TypeScript single-page application. Dependencies common to all labs:

| Layer | Technology |
|---|---|
| Build tool | Vite |
| UI framework | React 18 |
| Language | TypeScript |
| Styling | Tailwind CSS (semantic color tokens for dark-mode support) |
| Component primitives | Radix UI + shadcn-style components |
| Data visualization | Recharts (line charts) or custom inline SVG (gauges, AD-AS diagrams) |
| Animation | framer-motion |
| Server scaffold | Express (inert — no active routes) |
| Database scaffold | better-sqlite3 + Drizzle ORM (provisioned, not wired) |
| Deployment | Static `dist/public` served via Perplexity Computer Apps |

### Why a Monorepo of Independent Apps

Rather than a single multi-page application, each lab is built and deployed independently. This allows a single lab to be updated, rebuilt, and redeployed without touching any other lab's codebase or URL. It also keeps each `EconLab.tsx` self-contained — all question data, FRED series, and station logic are co-located in a single ~1,000–2,500 line file — which simplifies content editing.

### Representative Directory Tree

The following tree illustrates the structure of a typical lab (using `econ-lab-ch6` as the example):

```

├── client/
│   ├── src/
│   │   ├── pages/
│   │   │   └── EconLab.tsx          # All station logic, data, and quiz
│   │   ├── components/
│   │   │   └── ui/                  # shadcn-style Radix primitives
│   │   ├── hooks/

│   │   └── main.tsx
│   └── index.html
├── server/
│   ├── index.ts                     # Express entry point (stub)
│   └── routes.ts                    # Route registration (stub)
├── shared/
│   └── schema.ts                    # Drizzle ORM schema (provisioned, unused)
├── package.json
├── tsconfig.json
├── tailwind.config.ts
└── vite.config.ts
```

### Server and Database Status

The Express server (`server/index.ts`) starts and serves `dist/public` in production but registers no functional API routes. `routes.ts` is a stub. The SQLite database via `better-sqlite3` and `Drizzle ORM` is provisioned with a schema but no lab currently reads from or writes to it. All persistent state flows through `localStorage`. These scaffolds are available for future features (instructor analytics, cross-device sync).

### Color and Branding

Labs share a consistent color palette defined as Tailwind CSS variables:

```css
/* Primary navy */
--primary: #1e2d4a;

/* Accent amber */
--accent: #d97706;

/* Success green, warning amber, error red follow Tailwind defaults */
```

Dark-mode is supported throughout via Tailwind's semantic color tokens (`bg-card`, `text-foreground`, `border-border`), meaning labs automatically adapt to the user's system theme preference.

```
Cost-plus: Allowed Price = Accounting Costs + Normal Profit
Price cap: ceiling set years in advance; firm profits by cutting costs faster than cap declines
```

## 9. Assessment & Grading Workflow

The end-to-end workflow from a student's perspective:

1. **Student opens the Course Hub** (`econlab-hub/index.html` or `eco211-hub/index.html`). Labs with `localStorage` completion flags display `✓ Completed`; others show as available.

2. **Student works through stations.** Each station must be marked complete (interaction + "Mark Complete ✓" button). The Quiz nav pill displays a `🔒` lock icon until all content stations are marked complete.

3. **Student takes the quiz.** Fisher-Yates shuffles answer-option order on each attempt. Multi-select questions require exact-set match. Calculator stations in the quiz draw from the same numeric precision rules as the practice stations.

4. **Pass path (score ≥ mastery threshold):** Student is routed to the `ResultsScreen`. Key actions available:
   - View per-question review with explanations
   - Enter name in the name-input field (required to enable Print PDF)
   - Optionally write an exit-ticket response
   - Click "Print PDF" → generates a new browser tab via `window.open()` + `document.write()` + `window.print()` → student saves as PDF → submits to Brightspace
   - `localStorage.setItem("econlab_done_chN", "true")` is written; Course Hub will now show `✓ Completed`

5. **Fail path (score < mastery threshold):** Student is routed to the `NotYetScreen` (amber background). Key properties:
   - Displays score and the mastery threshold message
   - "This screen cannot be submitted — only the final Results screen counts"
   - "← Try the Quiz Again" button returns to `QuizStation` with a new shuffled draw
   - No station reset required — student can retry quiz directly

6. **Review labs** follow the same flow but with a 15-question quiz (13/15 mastery) and a section-score breakdown table on the Results screen that shows performance by topic area.

7. **Practice finals** have no mastery gate. All questions auto-advance after feedback is shown. The Results screen shows score/30 plus a chapter-by-chapter bar chart. The student still enters a name and prints a PDF for submission.

### Grade Scale (chapter labs)

| Score | Grade |
|---|---|
| 10/10 | A+ |
| 9/10 | A |
| 8/10 | B |
| 7/10 | C |
| < 7/10 | Needs Review |

## 11. Future Work / Known Limitations

- **Express server is inert.** `server/routes.ts` registers no functional API routes. The server scaffold exists for potential future features such as instructor-side analytics (quiz scores, time-on-station, retry counts) or server-generated exam PDFs with anti-tampering timestamps.

- **SQLite scaffold is present but unused.** `better-sqlite3` and Drizzle ORM are installed and a schema is provisioned, but no lab currently reads from or writes to the database. Wiring this up could enable per-student progress storage, class-level dashboards, and export of grade data to an LMS gradebook.

- **localStorage isolates progress per browser.** There is no cross-device sync. A student who completes a lab on a school computer will not see that completion on their phone. This is a structural limitation of the current client-only architecture.

- **No instructor admin panel.** Instructors cannot currently view which students have passed, how many retries they needed, or which questions are most frequently missed. This data would require a wired backend.

- **Ch2 and ECO 211 Ch2 are not yet linked in the hub dashboards.** Both labs are built and deployed but their URLs are not registered in the respective hub `index.html` files. They are accessible directly but do not appear as hub cards.

- **Review Lab Ch11–17 is not yet linked in the ECO 210 hub.** The lab is built (`econ-lab-review-ch1117/`) and can be deployed, but the hub URL entry is absent.

- **Screenshots are not embedded in this document.** Visual documentation of the UI could be added in a v2 pass using the `screenshot_page` tool to capture each lab's dashboard view.

- **Practice finals have no adaptive feedback.** The current design shows overall performance by chapter, but does not recommend specific stations for remediation. A simple rule engine (e.g., "score < 70% on Ch9 → revisit the CPI Math and Inflation History stations") could be added client-side.
